Visitor Analysis and Persona

Site: Heritage Rexburg

URL: Heritage website

Visitor Analysis

What are their demographics?

18-30 year olds, low-income, seeking a college degree, living in Rexburg, ID.

How did the site meet the needs of the visitor?

The site has a simple menu that is easy to navigate. By using the menu, the visitors are able to locate the information that they need.

Describe how the content was organized to facilitate it being found.

Besides the navigation menu, there are outlined, boxed links with one or two words describing the information that the link would take the visitor to.

What did the site do to make the mobile experience enjoyable (or at least not painful)?

The links are easy to navigate. The information is the right amount of text to get the message across, and the pictures are a good size. The site loads quickly.

Did the site seem to facilitate people in a mobile environment? If "Yes", how?

Yes. There isn’t a need to zoom in or out to view the content. It’s easy to find information.

Persona

Users of this site are between the ages of 18-25, or parents of people between the ages of 18-25. Their purpose in using the site is to learn more about Heritage apartments in Rexburg. The users want a site that is straightforward and easy to navigate. For example, users want to be able to quickly find information about prices and amenities.

Users want to see something modern and fresh. If it’s outdated, they will leave.

Persona Examples:

Carlie is starting her first semester at BYU-I. It is her first time moving away from home, so she wants to find an apartment that is a home away from home. She mostly uses her smart phone to research places she can live.

Meredith is a mother of twins attending BYU-I. She wants her kids to be safe and warm during the winter months. Meredith wants to locate an apartment complex that is a decent price and has a clean feel. She is an on-the-go mom, so she looks up approved housing on her mobile device while she waits at the doctor’s office.

Josh is going into his last year of school and wants to live somewhere close to campus. He wants a place that is social, so that he can meet lots of girls and take them on dates. He quickly browses through housing options on his phone.
